2019-2020

Novartis, a global leader in the pharmaceutical industry, aimed to bridge the knowledge gap for medical professionals by creating a centralized portal to offer both scientific content and easily consumable information.

Navigating this project brought its fair share of challenges. Firstly, I had the task of shaping a brand for the portal that would resonate with medical professionals, all while staying true to Novartis' established identity. Then came the coordination - balancing the efforts of two distinct developer teams, one based in Colombia and the other in Switzerland. Each team had its unique approach, and juggling the time zone differences added another layer of complexity. On top of that, the content had to be diverse, ranging from in-depth scientific articles to more accessible formats like podcasts, without diluting the core information.

To drive this project, I had to lead:

  • Brand Development: I spearheaded the brand's development for the portal, making sure it fit snugly with Novartis' values and resonated with our audience.
  • Team Synergy: Communication was key. By ensuring everyone was on the same page and setting straightforward goals, I managed to bridge our team in Colombia with Novartis' crew in Switzerland seamlessly.
  • Content Committee: Knowing the value of varied content, I pulled together a committee of creatives and doctors. We were all about keeping the content spot-on in terms of accuracy while mixing up its presentation styles.

Results

  • Engagement: Right out of the gate, when we launched the portal at a major medical event, we saw a wave of interest with over 2,000 sign-ups in just the first month.
  • Sustained Utility: It wasn't just a one-time thing. The portal still stands as a go-to resource for many medical pros, showcasing its lasting impact.
